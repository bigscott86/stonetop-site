# Stonetop Playbooks (Classes)

A **playbook** is Stonetop's version of a character class. It is a set of choices, prompts, and options that point toward a specific archetype while leaving room to make your character unique. The playbook also doubles as your character sheet, used to track stats, debilities, HP, XP, moves, and possessions.

**Choose one, no duplicates.** When you make a PC for Stonetop, the first thing you do is pick a playbook. Each table normally uses each of the nine standard playbooks at most once (no two players take the same one).

Every playbook opens with a choice of **three backgrounds** (the book labels this section "Background (Choose 1)"). Your background grants bonus moves, special possessions, or unique mechanics. After the background, each playbook then has you choose an **Instinct** (a one-line drive), an **Appearance**, and a **Place of origin and name**, before stepping through structured **Introductions** to weave the party together.

Common conventions across playbooks:
- **Starting stats** (all except the Would-be Hero): assign the array **+2, +1, +1, +0, +0, -1** across STR, DEX, CON, INT, WIS, CHA. When a debility is marked, you roll with disadvantage. The Would-be Hero is the exception (a weaker array, see below).
- **Debilities:** weakened, dazed, miserable.
- Each playbook lists a base **damage die**, **max HP**, and **special possessions** (usually "Pick 2").
- Note: the detailed move text, follower stats, and arcana live on the playbook *sheets* and *inserts* (pp. 141+); the descriptions captured here give move names, the starting-move loadout, and defining mechanics.

The nine standard playbooks (book order): The Blessed (p.105), The Fox (p.109), The Heavy (p.113), The Judge (p.117), The Lightbearer (p.121), The Marshal (p.125), The Ranger (p.129), The Seeker (p.133), The Would-be Hero (p.137).

## The Blessed

**Concept / role.** A true child of Danu, the Great Mother. The Blessed learns the secrets of the natural world and channels the Earth Mother's power, serving as the party's nature-priest, healer, and spirit-speaker. *"Danu, the Great Mother, provides. We need only learn her secrets... Her Blessed."*

**Book page:** 105 (description pp. 105–107).

**The three backgrounds:**
- **Initiate** — heir to Stonetop's sacred order of Danu; gains the **Rites of the Land** move and a band of fellow initiates as followers (see the *Initiates of Danu* insert; pick 2–3 from Enfys, Afon, Gwendyl, Olwin, Seren the Eldest).
- **Raised by Wolves** — grew up wild; gains **Trackless Step** and advantage when you Forage; mark XP when your wild ways alienate someone (once/session).
- **Vessel** — Danu's power has rooted in your soul; gains **Danu's Grasp**; you may lose 2d4 HP in place of spending 1 Stock (power at great cost).

**Starting stats:** +2, +1, +1, +0, +0, -1 (standard array).

**Starting HP / damage / armor / load:** HP max 18; damage **d6**; no armor granted by default.

**Signature & starting moves.** You start with **Spirit Tongue**, **Call the Spirits**, **1 from your Background**, and **1 of your choice**.
- *Spirit Tongue* — speak with beasts and spirits; always ask "What spirits are active here?"
- *Call the Spirits* — spend 1 Stock to make a place's/object's spirits manifest and hear you out.
- *Danu's Grasp* (Vessel) — spend 1 Stock, roll +WIS to restrain or damage a spirit/perversion of nature.
- *Rites of the Land* (Initiate) — once/season, hold Favor to spend in lieu of Stock; sacrifice to aid the steading.
- Other choosable moves include: Healer's Arts, Amulets & Talismans, Barkskin, Wards & Bindings, Veil, Into the Lion's Den, Heed My Words, Borrow Power, Trackless Step.

**Starting gear.** A **sacred pouch** (magical, holds up to 3 Stock) plus **pick 2** special possessions: Apiary, Collected offerings, Goatherd, Herb garden, or **Mastiffs** (2–3 followers; HP 6, d6 damage; cost: affection).

**Bonds / relationships.** Introductions ask about closest kin; whose heart & soul is entwined with yours; who taught you the secret ways; who is beloved by the goddess (your charge to nurture/guide/protect/heal). Cross-PC prompts cover sacred rites, blood-oaths, and who doubts Danu.

**Drive(s) — Instinct (choose 1):** Delight, Detachment, Nurture, Preservation, Reverence.

**Special mechanics / followers.** Sacred pouch and **Stock** economy; the Initiate background grants the **Initiates of Danu** followers; Mastiffs are an optional follower pick.

## The Fox

**Concept / role.** The clever, dangerous trickster — a skirmisher, scout, burglar, and social manipulator who plays to win. *"You want that Hedgehog? Go get a knife."*

**Book page:** 109 (description pp. 109–111).

**The three backgrounds:**
- **The Natural** — local prodigy good at everything; when you Seek Insight you may roll +INT instead of +WIS and add "What opportunity does no one else see?"
- **A Life of Crime** — a colorful criminal past; start with **Burgle** or **Light Fingers** (your choice) plus burglar tools or a hidden stash.
- **The Prodigal Returned** — a celebrity returnee with friends across the world; roll +CHA to call on a distant contact.

**Starting stats:** +2, +1, +1, +0, +0, -1 (standard array).

**Starting HP / damage / armor / load:** HP max 16; damage **d8**; no armor by default.

**Signature & starting moves.** You start with **Ambush OR Skill at Arms**; **Danger Sense OR Perceptive**; and **1 of your choice**.
- *Ambush* — get the drop on a foe; deal damage or roll +DEX for extra effects.
- *Skill at Arms* — roll +DEX (instead of +STR) to Clash.
- *Danger Sense* — ask "Is there an ambush or trap here?"
- *Perceptive* — ask 1 extra Seek Insight question (even on a 6-).
- Other choosable moves include: Catlike, Burgle, Light Fingers, Silver Tongued, Under Your Skin, Rapier Wit, Free Running, Parry & Riposte, All in the Wrist, Irresistible, Laugh at Danger.

**Starting gear.** Has a **sacred pouch** (holds up to 3 Stock) like the Blessed. **Pick 2** special possessions: Burglar's kit, Carpenter's tools, Distillery, Hidden stash, Mummer's kit, Scribe's tools, Tannery, Trade contacts.

**Bonds / relationships.** Includes **Tall tales** (build a couple of memorable misadventures). Introductions ask about closest kin; who holds the reins to your heart; whose respect means the world; to whom you owe an unrepayable debt; plus hijinx/trust prompts.

**Drive(s) — Instinct (choose 1):** Conscience, Freedom, Comfort, Prestige, Trickery.

## The Heavy

**Concept / role.** The bruiser and protector — the one who's not afraid to get bloody to stick up for good people. The party's frontline melee combatant and damage-soaker. *"Sometimes, good people need someone to stick up for them... someone like you."*

**Book page:** 113 (description pp. 113–115).

**The three backgrounds:**
- **Sheriff** — keeper of order; bark an order/warning and roll +CHA to make targets comply, dig in/flee, or attack.
- **Blood-Soaked Past** — a former name mothers used to scare children; Persuade with violence using +STR; deal +1d4 when you fight to kill.
- **Storm-Marked** — touched by Tor and bearing runic markings; start with the **Storm Markings** major arcanum.

**Starting stats:** +2, +1, +1, +0, +0, -1 (standard array).

**Starting HP / damage / armor / load:** HP max 20; damage **d10** (highest of the playbooks); armor depends on chosen moves/possessions (Armored move adds 2-armor body armor).

**Signature & starting moves.** You start with **Dangerous**, **Hard to Kill**, and **either Armored OR Uncanny Reflexes**.
- *Dangerous* — advantage when you deal your damage.
- *Hard to Kill* — at Death's Door, roll +CON or +nothing; mark a debility to regain 1 HP.
- *Armored* — carry a shield for reduced encumbrance; ignore cumbersome; (at start, add an iron hauberk / bronze cuirass / scale coat, 2 armor).
- *Uncanny Reflexes* — unarmored and lightly loaded, impose disadvantage on dodgeable damage.
- Other choosable moves include: Battle Joy, Berserker, Carved Out of Wood, Formidable, Payback, Relentless, Guardian, Frosty, Intimidating, Musclebound, Situational Awareness, Terror on the Field.

**Starting gear.** **Pick 2** special possessions: Distillery, Chirurgeon's tools, Husbandry tools, Smithy, Stoneworker's tools, or **Weapons of war** (choose up to 3: sword, battleaxe, warhammer, mace/flail, crossbow).

**Bonds / relationships.** Includes **A history of violence** (renowned deeds, things folks won't discuss, what keeps you up at night). Introductions ask about closest kin; lover/spouse/betrothed; who most needs protection; whose forgiveness you strive to earn; plus combat/trust prompts.

**Drive(s) — Instinct (choose 1):** Peace, Pride, Recklessness, Trouble, Violence.

## The Judge

**Concept / role.** Servant of Aratis (goddess of civilization), charged to keep the town: settle disputes, chronicle its tales, and defend it from chaos and ruin. A leader, lore-keeper, and anti-chaos warrior-priest. *"Take up your hammer, Judge. Your town needs you."*

**Book page:** 117 (description pp. 117–119).

**The three backgrounds:**
- **Legacy** — born and apprenticed into a long line of Judges; advantage to Know Things about Stonetop's people/history; pore over the Chronicle to learn answers.
- **Missionary** — sent by a larger order of Judges; add other Judges as neighbors; start with an **aviary** (messenger birds).
- **Prophet** — called personally by Aratis through dreams and visions; commune with Aratis (+WIS) for guidance and **Sanction**.

**Starting stats:** +2, +1, +1, +0, +0, -1 (standard array).

**Starting HP / damage / armor / load:** HP max 20; damage **d6**; armor via Armored move / symbol of authority.

**Signature & starting moves.** You start with **Censure**, **Chronicler of Stonetop**, plus **2 more of your choice**.
- *Censure* — denounce an individual as an agent of chaos; they become ashamed, doubtful, afraid, or enraged.
- *Chronicler of Stonetop* — share session notes to hold **Diligence**, spent to add +1 to any player's roll.
- Other choosable moves include: Castigate, The Hammer and the Book, Truth or Consequences, Bear Witness, Bulwark, Aegis of Faith, Vision Unclouded, Well-Read, Hound of Aratis, Knowledge is Power, Many Hands Make Light Work, For the Greater Good, Break Bread.

**Starting gear.** **Symbol of authority** (pick: Black iron maul / Makerglass shield / dark-ice Helm) plus **scribe's tools**, plus **pick 1** more: Aviary, Carpenter's tools, Engineer's tools, Smithy.

**Bonds / relationships.** Includes **The Chronicle** (describe its physical structure) and **The Lawkeeper** (Aratis's shrine and demands). Introductions ask about closest kin; lover/spouse/betrothed; your apprentice; the wisest town elder; plus discipleship/battle/judgement prompts.

**Drive(s) — Instinct (choose 1):** Ambition, Dispassion, Harmony, Orthodoxy, Zeal.

**Special mechanics.** Maintains **the Chronicle** (a physical place, mapped on the steading playbook); **Diligence** and (Prophet) **Sanction** pools.

## The Lightbearer

**Concept / role.** The appointed servant of Helior, God of Light — a hopeful, charismatic priest who literally wields holy light against creatures of darkness. *"Would you not bring that Light back into the darkness, to set your people free?"*

**Book page:** 121 (description pp. 121–123).

**The three backgrounds:**
- **Auspicious Birth** — born in Stonetop, marked by Helior; may mark this background's circle in place of a debility (clears on Make Camp/Convalesce).
- **Itinerant Mystic** — a wandering "kook"; hold **Enigma** (start with 3) spent to return when needed, Know Things, or Have What You Need.
- **Soul on Fire** — a worldly life transformed by Helior's light; Persuade a group by preaching charity/mercy/hope to spread your name or draw followers.

**Starting stats:** +2, +1, +1, +0, +0, -1 (standard array).

**Starting HP / damage / armor / load:** HP max 18; damage **d4** (lowest base die — a support/caster); armor via "A Candle Against the Dark" (2 armor when wielding holy light unarmored).

**Signature & starting moves.** You start with **Consecrated Flame** and **Invoke the Sun God**, plus **1 more of your choice**.
- *Consecrated Flame* — whisper consecration to make a flame cast holy light (uncomfortable for creatures of darkness).
- *Invoke the Sun God* — imbue holy light with Helior's power; choose a known **Invocation** and roll +WIS (see the *Invocations* insert).
- Other choosable moves include: A Candle Against the Dark, Purifying Flames, Piety, Radiant Countenance, Rise Like the Sun, Spring's First Thaw, All Is Illuminated, And Behold a Pale Horse, Keep the Home-Fires Burning, Guiding Light, Fire Within.

**Starting gear.** **Pick 2** special possessions: Apiary, Books & scrolls, Chandlery, Distillery, Glassworks, Holy relics, Luthier's tools.

**Bonds / relationships.** Includes **Praise the day** (Helior's worship, his shrine, your predecessor, how you came into your powers). Introductions ask about closest kin; who fans the flames of your heart; whose kindness warms your soul; who needs Helior's light; plus faith/guidance prompts.

**Drive(s) — Instinct (choose 1):** Charity, Hope, Mercy, Praise, Righteousness.

**Special mechanics.** Holy-light **Invocations** (Invocations insert); **Enigma** (Itinerant Mystic) and **Blessing** (Piety) pools.

## The Marshal

**Concept / role.** The militia commander and leader of people — runs the drills, mans the towers, and takes charge when things get bad, even at terrible cost. The party's tactician with a **Crew** of followers. *"That's the job, Marshal. You up for it?"*

**Book page:** 125 (description pp. 125–127).

**The three backgrounds:**
- **Scion** — descended from a long line; start with **Veteran Crew**; your Crew gains the *respected* tag.
- **Penitent** — former bandit/mercenary leader; Know Things via your bloody past (+STR); your Crew gains the *warriors* tag.
- **Luminary** — a natural, inspiring leader; start with **We Happy Few**; your Crew gains the *devoted* tag.

**Starting stats:** +2, +1, +1, +0, +0, -1 (standard array).

**Starting HP / damage / armor / load:** HP max 20; damage **d8**; armor via Armored move.

**Signature & starting moves.** You start with **Crew**, **Logistics**, **any moves from your Background**, and **1 move of your choice**.
- *Crew* — you command roughly six stalwart residents of Stonetop (see the *Crew* insert).
- *Logistics* — advantage on steading Muster / Pull Together / Requisition.
- *Veteran Crew* (Scion) — upgrade Crew tags, damage, or HP.
- *We Happy Few* (Luminary) — inspiring speech grants allies **Inspiration**.
- Other choosable moves include: Front Line Leader, Stentorian, Take the Measure, Speak Softly, Shake It Off, Shield Wall, Read the Land, Prepare a Welcome, Set-Up Strike, Sir Permission to Die Sir, Armored, Arts of War.

**Starting gear.** **Pick 2** special possessions: Chirurgeon's tools, Distillery, Engineer's tools, Personal symbol (grants Crew +1 Loyalty), Scribe's tools, or **Weapons of war** (choose up to 3: sword, long spear, battleaxe, composite bow).

**Bonds / relationships.** Includes **War stories** (the militia's last serious action). Introductions ask about closest kin; lover/spouse/betrothed; your lieutenant; whose kin died because of your decisions; plus crew/orders prompts.

**Drive(s) — Instinct (choose 1):** Authority, Caution, Drive, Honor, Ruthlessness.

**Special mechanics / followers.** Starts with a **Crew** (~6 followers; Crew insert), and command pools such as **Presence** (Front Line Leader) and **Command** (Stentorian).

## The Ranger

**Concept / role.** The wilderness scout and guardian — at home in the wild places beyond the Ringwall, guiding and protecting kith and kin against beasts and darker forces. *"Guide them, ranger, and keep them safe when darkness falls."*

**Book page:** 129 (description pp. 129–131).

**The three backgrounds:**
- **Mighty Hunter** — the best hunter in generations; start with **Expert Tracker** and **Stalker**.
- **Wide Wanderer** — widely travelled; start with **Mental Map**; add neighbors from across the world; Know Things about the wider world via +WIS.
- **Beast-Bonded** — soul bound to a wild beast; start with **Animal Companion**; mark companion actions usable at any distance.

**Starting stats:** +2, +1, +1, +0, +0, -1 (standard array).

**Starting HP / damage / armor / load:** HP max 18; damage **d8**; the **Pack Horse** move boosts load capacity.

**Signature & starting moves.** You start with **Home on the Range**, **any moves from your Background**, plus **1 of your choice**.
- *Home on the Range* — treat a 6- as a 7-9 when a journey makes you Defy Danger or Struggle as One.
- *Expert Tracker* / *Stalker* (Mighty Hunter), *Mental Map* (Wide Wanderer), *Animal Companion* (Beast-Bonded).
- Other choosable moves include: A Safe Place, Big Game Hunter, Blot Out the Sun, Call the Shot, Predator, Sniff Out Corruption, Survivalist, Warden of the Wild, Wild Speech, Naturalist, On the Hoof, Pathfinder.

**Starting gear.** Starts with a **composite bow** (far, +1 damage, x piercing; low ammo, all out) plus **pick 2** more: Distillery, Hideouts, Husbandry tools, **Hounds** (2–3 followers; HP 6, d6), Lay of the land, Trapping gear.

**Bonds / relationships.** Includes **Something wicked this way comes** (the outside threat you fear). Introductions ask about closest kin; to whom you always return; who would be lost without you; who has much to learn from you; plus wonder/wanderlust prompts.

**Drive(s) — Instinct (choose 1):** Adventure, Independence, Stewardship, Tenacity, Wonder.

**Special mechanics / followers.** The Beast-Bonded background grants an **Animal Companion** (Animal Companion insert); Hounds are an optional follower pick.

## The Seeker

**Concept / role.** The scholar-adventurer who plunges into the unknown, grasping for lost knowledge and dangerous magic. The party's arcanist, lore-master, and wielder of **arcana**. *"What will you find, o Seeker? Signs of a bright new age? Or signs of our doom?"*

**Book page:** 133 (description pp. 133–135).

**The three backgrounds (each starts you with a major arcanum):**
- **Patriot** — embraced dark power to protect your family; start with **Let's Make a Deal**, **Well Versed in the Things Below**, and 1 major arcanum (Hec'tumel Codex, Red Scepter, or Staff of the Lidless Orb).
- **Antiquarian** — digging up the buried past; start with **Polyglot**, **Well Versed in the Makers**, and 1 major arcanum (Noruba's Ice Sphere, Azure Hand, or Mindgem).
- **Witch Hunter** — dedicated to destroying horrors; start with **Everything Bleeds**, **Well Versed** in (pick 1) the Fae / the Things Below / the Last Door, and 1 major arcanum (Demonhide Cloak, Redwood Effigy, or Twisted Spear).

**Starting stats:** +2, +1, +1, +0, +0, -1 (standard array).

**Starting HP / damage / armor / load:** HP max 16; damage **d6**; no armor by default.

**Signature & starting moves.** You start with **Well Versed**, **Work With What You've Got**, plus **1 from your Background**.
- *Well Versed* — mark topics of expertise; ask a free follow-up when you Know Things about them.
- *Work With What You've Got* — roll +INT to use the environment to harm/impede foes.
- Background moves: *Let's Make a Deal*, *Polyglot*, or *Everything Bleeds*.
- Other choosable moves include: Attuned, Conduit of Power, Countermeasures, Everything Burns, Quick Study, Safety First, Sage Advice, Logbook, Magpie, Never at a Loss, Cryptologist, Initiate of the Secret Arts.

**Starting gear.** Starts with **scribe's tools** plus **pick 2** more: Books & scrolls, Distillery, Engineer's tools, Laboratory (produces naphtha), Paraphernalia, Trade contacts.

**Bonds / relationships.** Includes **Collection** (major and minor arcana: where acquired, who wants it, what it cost; draw 3 Minor Arcana cards). Introductions ask about closest kin; spouse/lover/betrothed; whom you trust more than yourself; whom you secretly watch over; plus discovery/secrets prompts.

**Drive(s) — Instinct (choose 1):** Cunning, Curiosity, Hubris, Mystery, Vision.

**Special mechanics.** **Arcana** — starts with **1 major arcanum** (partly unlocked) plus minor arcana (see the Arcana inserts/cards); pools such as **Protection** (Safety First).

## The Would-be Hero

**Concept / role.** The destined underdog — not like most people, on a path to adventure and greatness, if they live long enough to prove it. A flexible, fate-favored character that grows into power. *"There's greatness in you. Let's hope you live long enough for everyone else to see it."*

**Book page:** 137 (description pp. 137–139).

**The three backgrounds:**
- **Impetuous Youth** — longs for excitement; "give it your all" to bump a roll up one tier (6-→7-9, 7-9→10+, 10-11→12+) at a cost.
- **Driven** — burdened with terrible purpose by some event; always able to **Burn Brightly** (spend 2 XP after a roll to add +1).
- **Destined** — fate has marked you; choose your destiny's terms, roll **+Omens** at session start for visions; until your destiny is fulfilled, treat a 6- on Death's Door as 7-9 and a 7-9 as 10+.

**Starting stats:** **+1, +0, +0, +0, +0, -1** — note this is a **weaker** array than every other playbook (which use +2, +1, +1, +0, +0, -1). The Would-be Hero instead grows via the **Potential for Greatness** move.

**Starting HP / damage / armor / load:** HP max 16; damage **d6**; armor via possessions (e.g., the tannery's boiled leather cuirass).

**Signature & starting moves.** You start with **Anger is a Gift**, **Potential for Greatness**, and **2 other moves of your choice**.
- *Anger is a Gift* — burn with righteous anger to hold **Resolve**, spent for bursts of courage, surprise, inspiration, or +damage.
- *Potential for Greatness* — once per level, on a stat-roll 10+, mark a growth (stat +1 to max +2, +4 max HP, or upgrade damage die to d8); this is how the Would-be Hero catches up.
- Other choosable moves include: Speak Truth to Power, Better Part of Valor, I Get Knocked Down / But I Get Up Again, Resourceful, Underestimated, Up With People, Iron Will, Inquiring Minds, Never Gonna Keep Me Down, Tough Love, Something to Remember Me By.

**Starting gear.** **Pick 2** special possessions: A heap of expectations (useless), **A good dog** (follower; HP 6, d6), Husbandry tools, Smithy, Stoneworker's tools, **Personal token** (shield/cloak/letter/flute/locket/tinder box), Tannery.

**Bonds / relationships.** Introductions ask about closest kin; place/name/origin; and party-weaving prompts (via the shared Introductions framework on the sheet). Move-driven bonds include *Up With People* (Rapport) and *Tough Love*.

**Drive(s) — Instinct (choose 1):** Defiance, Doubt, Earnestness, Optimism, Sacrifice.

**Special mechanics.** Asterisked level-6 moves: the first time you use any move marked `*`, **cross off "Would-be"** on the front page (you become simply "the Hero"). Pools include **Resolve** (Anger is a Gift) and (Destined) **Omens**.
